Crypto Billing

Verified Payment Instructions Only

If a customer or owner-authorized agent wants to settle in crypto, the safest path is to use invoice-confirmed or intent-confirmed payment instructions. That keeps settlement flexible, values non-default assets against dollars cleanly, and avoids publishing wallet details broadly on the public site.

  • Preferred recurring asset: USDC
  • Preferred network: Base
  • Supported rails: Base by default, with other assets or networks handled only by invoice or agreement.
  • Dollar rule: non-default payments must map to a clear USD-equivalent amount before fulfillment.
  • Safety rule: payment instructions are issued per buyer, per invoice, or per verified payment intent.
  • Why Triton differs from Clearance: Triton is a custom pilot and subscription sale, so it stays sales-led. Fixed-price products like Clearance can publish a public payment identity because price, asset, and verification rules are standardized.
  • Release rule: access ships only after funds are confirmed, spendable, and manually approved.

Owner Rights

How Bots Spend Money Without Going Rogue

Agents should be allowed to move fast, but only inside a policy created by the human who owns the wallet, the budget, and the consequences.

  • Budget ceiling: owner defines the max amount an agent can spend per billing cycle.
  • Allowed products: the agent can only buy or renew subscriptions whitelisted by the owner.
  • Renewal policy: auto-renew, remind, or manual approval can be set per subscription.
  • Audit trail: every purchase records who authorized it, what policy was used, and when renewal occurs.
  • Escalation rule: anything outside policy routes back to the owner for approval.
